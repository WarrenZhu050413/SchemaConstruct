‚ö° Judgement Drivers ‚ñ∂

Suspicious Cues

  • Embedded Links: 17 participants treat any URL as a red flag; tinyurl, http://, multi-link messages raise alarms.
  • Format & Grammar: 15 flag misspellings, odd casing, or character substitutions; urgency language ("reply in 4 hours") triggers skepticism.
  • Sender Trust: 18 review numbers, yet only 11 deem unknown senders suspicious; unfamiliar area codes and email-style senders unsettle users.
  • Context Mismatch: 6 reject texts unrelated to current activity; urgent directives without context push 4 to dismiss outright.

Legitimacy Cues

  • Personalization: Card last-four, named contacts, or tailored balance figures convince 14 participants.
  • Expected Event: 11 trust texts tied to known deliveries or transactions.
  • Recognized Sender: Saved contacts or branded short codes sway 10, yet some remain wary of 5-digit codes.
  • Official Formatting: 8 rely on structured templates, app mentions, or security symbols (Android padlock) as authenticity anchors.
üìä Threat Patterns & Impact ‚ñ∂

Prevalent Scam Themes

  • Logistics Hooks: 44% received fake USPS/UPS updates; timing around holidays increases clicks, and P11 reported a near-miss when awaiting a parcel.
  • Financial Alerts: 36% saw spoofed bank or bill notices; proactive calls to banks (e.g., P25) validate suspicions.
  • Commercial Lures: 28% encountered dubious sales; stylized characters and vague imagery (P16) trigger doubt.
  • Impersonation & Offers: 24% saw wrong-number or gift-card ploys; emotional manipulation and social familiarity drive risk.

Consequences & Confusions

  • Spam vs. Scam Blur: Participants conflate legit politics or promos with fraud due to inconsistent sender IDs.
  • Under-Reporting: Fear of effort without payoff keeps most from escalating issues.
  • Training Gap: Younger users without formal awareness misclassify legit SMS, while trained peers become hyper-cautious (false positives on real alerts).
üìç Verification & Reporting Workflow ‚ñ∂

Current Behaviors

  • Official Channels: 21 confirm via apps, call centers, or websites before acting.
  • Social Reliance: 2 consult family gatekeepers; 1 uses tools like SpyDialer; 1 experiments with ChatGPT for link vetting.
  • No Verification: 2 ignore or delete without checks, assuming filters will block repeats.

Reporting Barriers

  • Lack of Feedback: No participant received follow-up after reporting via carrier or iOS "Report Junk".
  • Friction Points: Android warnings educate but hide reporting; iOS lacks pre-emptive cues entirely.
  • Motivation: Users report only when harm feels imminent (financial exposure, workplace risk).
üèõÔ∏è Design & Policy Opportunities ‚ñ∂

Interface Enhancements

  • iOS Parity: Bring Android-style warning banners, explainers, and padlock cues to iMessage.
  • One-Tap Reporting: Surface "Report spam" next to warnings; auto-share context with carriers.
  • Contextual Labels: Distinguish marketing subscriptions from potential scams to reduce false alarms.

Education & Policy Levers

  • Scenario Training: Target younger cohorts with micro-lessons on personalization traps and package ploys.
  • Feedback Loop: Provide status updates after reports to reinforce reporting habit.
  • Carrier Collaboration: Align short-code vetting and branding so legitimate outreach is visually distinct.
