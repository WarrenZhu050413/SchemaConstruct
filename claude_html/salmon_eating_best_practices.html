‚ñ∂SAFETY Food Safety & Handling

Critical Safety Rules

‚ö†Ô∏è NEVER eat raw salmon unless it's been:
  • Labeled "sushi-grade" or "sashimi-grade"
  • Frozen at -4¬∞F (-20¬∞C) for 7 days OR -31¬∞F (-35¬∞C) for 15 hours (kills parasites)
  • Purchased from reputable fishmonger/restaurant

Storage Guidelines

  • Fresh/Raw: 1-2 days in coldest part of fridge (32-34¬∞F)
  • Cooked: 3-4 days in airtight container
  • Frozen: 2-3 months at 0¬∞F (-18¬∞C)
  • Thawing: In fridge overnight, never room temp

Contamination Prevention

  • Wash hands before/after handling
  • Use separate cutting boards for fish
  • Don't cross-contaminate with other foods
  • Clean surfaces with hot soapy water
  • Cook within 2 hours of reaching room temp

Wild vs. Farmed Salmon

üåä Wild-Caught

  • Pros: Leaner, firmer texture, natural diet, higher Omega-3 per calorie, often more sustainable
  • Cons: More expensive, seasonal availability, potential mercury exposure (low but present)
  • Best Choice: Alaskan wild salmon (strictly managed)
  • Labels: Look for MSC (Marine Stewardship Council) certification

üè≠ Farmed

  • Pros: Year-round availability, lower cost, fattier/milder taste
  • Cons: Higher contaminants (PCBs), antibiotics, environmental concerns, fed grain/fish meal
  • Best Choice: Norwegian or Faroese farmed (better standards)
  • Labels: ASC (Aquaculture Stewardship Council) or organic certifications
Sustainability Tip: Prioritize Alaskan wild-caught (sockeye, coho) or responsibly farmed Atlantic salmon with ASC/BAP certification. Avoid endangered species like certain King salmon populations.

‚ñ∂ Dining Etiquette & Practical Tips

How to Eat Salmon Properly

  • Deboning: Run fork along spine/ribs to detect pin bones, remove with tweezers or fingers
  • Skin: Crispy skin is edible and delicious; peel off if soft/rubbery
  • Temperature: Let rest 3-5 minutes after cooking for even heat distribution
  • Portion Size: 4-6 oz (113-170g) per serving is standard
  • Utensils: Use fork and knife; break into flakes along natural grain

Restaurant Ordering Tips

  • Ask for preparation method and species (wild vs. farmed)
  • Request doneness preference (medium is usually best)
  • Inquire about sustainable sourcing if not labeled
  • At sushi restaurants, order "sake" (salmon) nigiri or sashimi
‚ñ∂ Common Mistakes to Avoid

üö´ Don't Make These Errors

  • Overcooking: Salmon dries out quickly above 145¬∞F‚Äîuse thermometer
  • Not patting dry: Moisture prevents browning and crisping
  • Cooking cold: Let come to room temp for 15-20 min before cooking
  • No seasoning: Salt enhances natural flavor‚Äîseason generously
  • Flipping too much: Let develop crust before turning (one flip only)
  • Buying pre-cut portions: Whole sides are fresher, cut your own
  • Ignoring smell test: Fresh salmon should NOT smell "fishy"
